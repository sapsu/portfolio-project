About me

My first touch with coding was on 8th grade, in the 90's. I was one of the few girls in our school to enrol in the optional computer science course. We learned to build our own website in html. After that, many years passed and I built a career in health care. A few years ago, I became more interested in learning how to build websites and applications. The health tech boom and difficult times during COVID influenced this. The fundamentals of my own profession changed over night, and video appointments became a part of my every workday. I was fascinated by the possibilities in health tech.

I began to study open university courses and coding workshops, that had become readily available online during lockdown. Recently, I graduated from SheCodes, which is an amazing online front-end development bootcamp for women. In the future, my dream is to continue learning, helping and making people's lives better, but this time using my new skillset as a front-end developer. I'm also interested in UI/UX design (I love designing websites and logos using Figma). When I'm not coding, I enjoy travelling, hiking, watching movies and sipping good coffee in cozy caf√©s.
