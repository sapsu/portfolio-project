About me

My first touch with coding was on 8th grade, in the 90's. I was one of the few girls in our school to enrol in the voluntary computer science course. The teacher taught us to build our own website in html.

After that, many years passed and I built a career in health care. A few years ago I became more interested in learning how to build websites and applications. The health tech boom and difficult times during COVID influenced this. The fundamentals of my own profession changed over night, and video appointments became a part of my every workday. I was fascinated by the possibilities and began to take part in open university courses and workshops, that had become readily available online during lockdown.

Recently, I began my journey with SheCodes, which is an amazing company offering coding workshops for women. In the future, my dream is to continue learning, helping and making people's lives better, but this time using my new skillset as a developer. I'm interested in UI/UX design and front-end web development. Working for a digital health business would be the best thing that I could hope for.
